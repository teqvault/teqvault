About Teq Vault

A multi-disciplinary innovation studio operating at the intersection of interactive software, digital storytelling, and engineering.

The Founder

Jude Mensah Nana Baffoe founded Teq Vault LLC in 2026 in Bossier City, Louisiana. By trade, he is a licensed electrician — a background that instilled a deep, practical understanding of systems, circuits, and the consequences of imprecise engineering.

Alongside his electrical career, Jude pursued a Computer Science degree at Southern New Hampshire University, specializing in computational graphics and visualization. It was this combination — hands-on hardware knowledge and formal software training — that gave Teq Vault its defining edge: the ability to think from the circuit up.

Teq Vault began as a solo operation. Every game, every line of code, every manuscript was produced by one person building an ecosystem from scratch — before hiring, before funding, before infrastructure. The creative output that exists today is proof of that foundation.

Origin & Mission

Teq Vault was established on a singular, uncompromising principle: Engineering Worlds, Not Just Games.

Historically, the entertainment and technology sectors have been siloed. Game developers build software, authors write books, engineers design hardware. Teq Vault was built to shatter these boundaries. The most profound consumer experiences happen when these disciplines are vertically integrated from day one.

Our mission is to bridge the gap between imagination and engineering — creating immersive digital worlds, compelling literature, real-world software products, and the physical technology that brings them all to life.

The Teq Vault Methodology

Our approach to product development is highly systematic. Unlike traditional studios that begin with a game prototype and graft a story onto it retroactively, our pipeline begins in the Literary Division.

We architect transmedia universes — like the rich organic world of Eyuforyia or the theological expanses of Aeterna Divisa — through full-length manuscripts and story bibles first. These literary assets serve as immutable blueprints. Once the lore, physics, factions, and character motivations are narratively sound, we hand these blueprints to the Interactive Entertainment Division.

This ensures that when we build a mobile-first match-3 RPG like Quetiemals, the mechanics aren't arbitrary — they are deeply rooted in a canonized universe. Running in parallel is our Software Division, which builds utility platforms like TeqXus AI and TeqDocs that demonstrate engineering range and generate revenue that sustains the studio's creative work.

Looking to the Future

While software and intellectual property form the foundation of our current market position, our long-term roadmap extends into the physical realm. The final pillar of the Teq Vault ecosystem is our Electronics R&D Division.

We are actively researching proprietary hardware — from embedded tactical simulators to specialized physical interfaces — designed to interact with our proprietary software environments. By controlling the entire stack, from narrative lore to the circuit boards rendering it, Teq Vault aims to deliver an unparalleled tier of digital immersion.
